Dhaka is the capital and largest city of Bangladesh. It is one of the most densely populated cities in the world.
Key landmarks include the Lalbagh Fort, a 17th-century Mughal fort complex, and the Ahsan Manzil, the former residential palace of the Nawab of Dhaka.
The city is famous for its rickshaws, with an estimated half a million rickshaws running on its streets daily.
Dhaka's cuisine is known for dishes like Biryani, Kachchi, and various Pithas (rice cakes). Old Dhaka is a food lover's paradise.
The main river flowing by Dhaka is the Buriganga River.
Dhaka is the center of the country's political, cultural, and economic life.
Notable educational institutions include the University of Dhaka, Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET), and Dhaka Medical College.
Traffic congestion is a major issue in the city.
Key shopping areas include New Market, Jamuna Future Park, and Bashundhara City.
